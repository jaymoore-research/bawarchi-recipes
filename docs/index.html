The Bawarchi Recipe Collection

Home recipes sent in by readers of bawarchi.com, c. 2001–2008, recovered from the Internet Archive. About this collection

About this collection

In the early 2000s, bawarchi.com ran a Readers' Contributions section: home cooks across India and the diaspora sent in their own recipes, each with the cook's name. The site is long gone, and these recipes were never in any book. This is that collection, rebuilt.

Recovered from the Wayback Machine

Every recipe here was pulled from the Internet Archive's snapshots of bawarchi.com. Nothing was rewritten: the ingredients and method are exactly as the original cooks submitted them, quirks and all.

The snapshots ("freezes")

The archive froze the site at different moments, and each freeze captured a different slice:

  • 2001 — the main freeze. Clean, complete pages; the bulk of the collection and the source of the original category listings.
  • ~2004 — a fallback freeze. When a recipe's newest snapshot was a broken stub, the recovery reached back to the closest 2004 capture instead.
  • 2007–2008 — the "sify" era, after bawarchi moved to sify.com. Many of these later freezes truncated mid-page before the recipe, so they were only kept when they still carried the full method.

How complete is it?

5,806 recipes — about 99% of every recipe the archive still holds. Around 76 were only ever frozen as truncated stubs and can't be fully recovered; they're left out rather than shown half-finished. Categories come from the site's own index pages, filled in with keyword matching for later recipes the indexes never listed.

Recipes are readers' own and were never tested by bawarchi — cook with a little judgement.
