Avial

Ingredients

To Grind:

Coconut - 1 - Grated
Jeera - 3 tsp
Green Chillies - 10
Curry Leaves - Little

Seasoning:

Coconut Oil/Refined Oil* - 1 tbsp
Mustard Seeds - 4 tsp
Curry Leaves - Little
* Note: Do not use Sesame Oil

Vegetables:

Pumpkin* - Cut into medium thick long pieces - 2 bowls
Beans - Cut into long pieces - 1 bowl
Carrot - Cut into long pieces - 1 bowl
Aaloo - Cut into medium thick long pieces - 1 1/2 bowls
Green Peas - 1 bowl
Drum Stick - Cut into long pieces - 2
Avaraikai / Teapods - cut into long pieces - 1 bowl
Snake Gourd - Cut into long pieces - 1 bowl
* You can use Chou-Chou (Bangalore Kathirikai) for pumpkin.
Other Items:

Curd - Preferably Sour Curd - 3 bowls minimum.
Salt to taste

Method:

  1. Cook all the vegetables with 1 to 2 tbsp curds* and salt to taste. (2 whistles if cooker is used).
  2. Grind the ingredients.
  3. Heat oil in a deep fry pan and add seasoning items.
  4. Add boiled vegetables and ground coconut, etc., and boil for 3 to 4 minutes.
  5. Add curds and boil for 5 to 10 minutes in medium flame. **
  6. Avial is ready to serve.

Avial and Adai combination is the one South Indians are crazy about. Avial is good with Sambar rice , Vegetable Pullao and with Ghee rice.

* Curds will keep the vegetables colours to some extent.
** If Avial is a little watery, add some rice flour when it is boiling. This will increase the consistency.
