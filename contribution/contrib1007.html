Ingredients

Ash pumpkin / Tomato / Yam
Colacasia (Chembu / Arbi)
Banana (Kerala variety)
Mangalore (Yellow) Cucumber / Mango - 200 to 250 gms.
Coconut powder - 2 cups
Green chilies - 10 to 15 nos.
Sour Curds - 2 to 3 cups
Turmeric powder - 1/4 tsp.
Salt - to your taste
Mustard seeds - a pinch
Fenugreek (Vendayam / Methi) seeds - a pinch
Red Chilies - 1 or 2
Curry leaves (Karipatha) - a few

Method :

  1. First cut the vegetable (whichever one you choose), in whatever shape you want it.
  2. Wash it thoroughly (anything other than tomatoes) and put it in a vessel.
  3. Add turmeric powder, salt and water (just enough to immerse the vegetable).
  4. Grind the coconut powder with green chilies and sour curds.
  5. When the vegetables are cooked, add the coconut-curds mix.
  6. Let it boil very lightly (it should not overboil). Keep aside.
  7. Saute the mustard seeds, fenugreek seeds, red chilies and curry leaves together.
  8. Use this sauted mixture for garnishing the more-kootaan.
  9. Serve hot / cold with steamed rice. Also goes well with chappathis.

    Note : It is advisable not to reheat this preparation as there is always the possibility of it curdling and spoiling the taste. Store in refrigerator when not in use.

    Maelagu Kozhambu (Spicy Gravy):

    Ingredients :

    Thoovar Dal - 1 tsp.
    Channa Dal - 1 tsp.
    Urud Dal - 1 tsp.
    Coriander seeds - 2 tsp.
    Asafetida - 2 pinches
    Cumin - 1 tsp.
    Dry Red Chilies - 6 nos.
    Curry Leaves - 1 handful
    Tamarind - small lime size ball
    Mustard seeds - 1/4 tsp.
    Salt - to your taste

    Method :

    1. Heat a little oil in saucepan.
    2. Saute the thoovar dal, channa dal, urud dal, coriander, asafetida, cumin and red chilies.
    3. Add the curry leaves.
    4. Grind all these to a paste in a mixer and keep aside.
    5. Soak the tamarind in hot water for about 5 minutes and then repeatedly squeeze out as much juice out of it.
    6. Mix the ground paste to the tamarind juice, add salt and let it boil till it becomes quite thick.
    7. Fry some mustard seeds and add to the mixture.
    8. Serve hot in small servings. Best with plain steamed rice.

    Warning : This is quite a spicy preparation. Not recommended for weak stomachs.
