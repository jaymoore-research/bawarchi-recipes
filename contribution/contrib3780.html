I am giving Rice uppuma (arisi uppuma) which is my favourite dish. Also, it is very easy to do and a change from rest of the uppumas.

E-mail : rama_sep@hotmail.com

Ingredients

Rice 1 cup
Thoor dhal 3/4 tsp, Moong dhal 1 tbsp
Red Chillies 2 or 3
Jeera 1/2 tsp
Pepper 5 or 6
Curry Leaves
Ghee optional
Vegetables, Potato, Carrot and Cabbage

You can do this dish with or without vegetables.

Method:

  1. Fry rice, dhals, jeera and pepper in an empty vanali till it is hot and grind them into coarse pieces.
  2. Season mustard, urad dhal, channa dhal, asafoetida in oil and fry the rice for few mts and add salt, water (for 1 cup rice, take 2 1/2 cups water) and stir well, keep it inside the cooker and steam them. If you want to add veg., before adding rice, add the veg., (chop into lengthy pieces) stir for few min and as usual.
  3. Once it is done, you can sprinkle some coconut and mix with ghee. It gives better taste.

If you want to get the best taste, you should do this using vengala panai but, now, everyone start doing this with cooker itself. Serve hot with Coconut Chutney or Onion Gojju.

Poritha Kuzhambu:

This is a very tasty one which my friend gave this recipe.

1 handful Moongdhal, 1 handful Black Channa dhal, vegetables like Asparagus, Plantain, Cluster Beans, Sambhar Powder, Salt, Tamarind.

For seasoning: Oil with Mustard and Curry Leaves.

Method:

  1. Chop the veg., into small pieces -just we do for curry.
  2. Soak black channa dhal in water overnight and boil it with salt, keep aside.
  3. Boil the veg., and moong dhal separately with little salt. Mash moong dhal well. Do the seasoning and add water with tamarind, sambhar powder, salt and veg., with channa. Allow it to boil well. Finally, add the mashed moong dhal and allow it to boil for 2 min.
  4. Serve hot with rice.

For cluster beans, instead of adding this channa, you can add the boiled bengal gram dhal and instead of moong dhal, you can add boiled thoor dhal. Rest are same.
