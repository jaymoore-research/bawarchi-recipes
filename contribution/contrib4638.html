The badies which I have referred actually adds to the taste of the Tahiri which also makes it different from the other sorts of fried rice. Tahiri

Ingredients

2 cups Rice (preferably long grained) soaked for 20 min
2 Small Onions properly diced
2 Medium sized Tomatoes cut into cubes
1 inch Ginger grated
1 Capsicum cut into small pieces
4 cups of Water
1 1/2 Cup freshly cut vegetables (Cauliflowers, French Beans diced, 1 Carrot cut into small pieces, Green Peas (if there is no green peas you can have soaked black gram), Potato diced into small pieces, and carrot diced (optional). The combination can be varied according to the taste
Oil to cook
Homemade Badies
Cumin seeds (1/2 teaspoon)
Coriander Powder (1 teaspoon)
Salt To taste
Turmeric Powder (1/2 teaspoon)
Green Chillies 2-3
Garam Masala (1 teaspoon)

Method:

  1. Heat oil in a pan or a cooker. Put cut green chillies into the oil. Add Cumin seeds and let it splutter.
  2. Add home made badies in the oil and fry till it gets brown. Now add Ginger (Grated) into the pan and fry it till it becomes lightly transparent.
  3. Add the diced onions into the pan and let it become transparent. Add the freshly cut vegetables and fry it.
  4. Add soaked rice into the pan and stir it, immediately add coriander powder, salt, turmeric powder garam masala into the pan and stir it for next 5 -7 min utes. Add capsicum and tomatoes at this stage and stir for another 2 min then add four cups of water and cook till the water evaporates or the way the rice is cooked.

I hope you will like the preparation if you follow the instructions properly. I am not very sure about the timings but I usually prepare and it tastes really good.
