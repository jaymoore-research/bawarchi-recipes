Adai is a South-Indian pancake different from Dosai in the sense that it is prepared of Tur Sal, Chana Dal and Rice. But one can always try a variation that tastes good enough! I produce a recipe for Protein-Rich Adai for which you can use up the left-over pulses lying in your freezer or cupboard! Children really love it. An easy way to make your fussy-eaters have a good protein-rich diet!

Protein-Rich Adai

Ingredients

2 cups of Tur Dal / Moong Dal / Kabuli Chana / Chana Dal / Chana / Mattar / Any other shelled pulses or a combination of whatever lentils you have lying around in the house!
2) 1 cup Rice
3) 1/4th cup Udad dal (optional)
4) 5 to 6 Red Chillies
5) 1" Ginger
6) 2 Green Chillies
7) A pinch of Hing
8) Curry Leaves
9) Salt to taste

Method:

  1. Soak the dal and rice separately in water for at least 2 hours (even overnight will do)
  2. Wet grind coarsely with Red chillies, ginger, green chillies and hing to form a thick batter. Add salt to the batter (as per taste).
  3. Tear the curry leaves to bits and mix into the batter.
  4. In a flat iron tava, put a few drops of oil and let the tava heat up. Spread the oil evenly over the tava.
  5. Take a tablespoonful of the Adai batter and spread it out on the tava like a Dosa - only you have to spread it thicker than a dosa.
  6. Take a spoon of oil and pour it around the edges.
  7. Roast on low fire and turn the Adai onto the other side when golden brown.
  8. Serve with Ketchup, Jaggery or White Butter.

A Variation:

If you want to experiment with a more interesting taste, you could also add your leftover sabji alongwith some Methi leaves into the batter and spread out the Adai! Children also love the taste if you add the Maggi Noodle Tastemaker masala to the batter before roasting it.
