I am Lalita Iyer living in Bangalore, the Garden City of India. I will give you a very old recipe originating from Kerala dating back to the late 18th Century. It is called the Purwalankai. It is very nutritious and low calorie form of Ladoo. It is very good for growing children. I remember my great grandmother giving us when we were a child.

Purwalankai

Ingredients

2 Cup Wheat
1 cup whole Green Gram
1 cup Rice
4 cup Jaggery
1 tsp Dry Ginger powder
10 - 15 pieces of Cardamom (elaichi) powder
1/2 Coconut cut into 1/2 inch pieces

Method:

  1. Roast wheat, gram & rice separately till they turn light brown on a low flame.
  2. Grind the three together into a fine powder and sieve them.
  3. Add jaggery in 1/2 cup of water, cook it to make a syrup. To ensure it is properly done test little syrup to see if it sinks in a cup of plain water, if yes then the syrup is done well.
  4. Remove the syrup from flame and add cardamom & dry ginger powder, sliced coconuts and 90% of the fine powder.
  5. Roll this mixture into small balls of required size and roll them in the balance 10% powder. Cool the balls, it is now ready to eat.

Traditionally this turns out very hard after cooling and then people use to crack it against the door panel maybe that is why it is called the PURWALANKAI.
