Masala Arvi

Ingredients

Arvi (medium size) 1/2 kg
Onions large 2
Tomatoes (medium) 2
Tamrind (pulp) 1/2 cup
Coconut grated 2 tbs
Cardamoms 3
Bay leaves 2
Curry leaves 10 to 12
Mustard seeds 1/2 tsp
Cumin seeds 1/2 tsp
Fenugreek seeds 1/4 tsp
Salt to taste
Groundnut powder 1 tbsp
Ginger Garlic paste 1 tbsp
Chilli powder 1 tsp
Turmeric powder 1/2 tsp
Coriander powder 1 tsp
Cumin powder (jeera) 1 tsp
Garam masala 1 tsp
Chopped Coriander half handfull
Water 2 to 3 cups of water
Oil 3 tbsp

Method:

  1. Boil the arvi and peel the skin and cut into 2" length wise or breath wise and keep aside (do not over boil them otherwise they get pulpy). Cut the onions into thin slices, add 2 cardamoms, 2 bay leaves and boil in enough of water till the onions become transparent, seive the excess water and let it cool for sometime and then grind along with grated coconut untill smooth in a mixy.
  2. Heat Oil add cumin seeds, mustard seeds and fenugreek seeds. Let it splutter, then add curry leaves and stir for few seconds. Add the onion coconut paste and stir and let it cook for a minute and then add ginger garlic paste, stir and cook till oil separtes.
  3. Now add the chopped tomatoes and groundnut powder, Salt, chilli powder, turmeric powder, corainder powder, jeera powder and stir for some time and let it cook for 2 to 3 min and now add 2 cups of water and stir for few seconds and let it come to a boil.
  4. When the gravy comes to a boil add tamrind pulp and stir, and let it cook, (NOTE: if you want more gravy you can add another cup of water now and then let it cook for a min and check for the salt and tamrind juice, if its less sour you can add some more pulp.) and now add the boiled arvis and chopped coriander. and cook till the gravy is thick.

Tips:

  1. If more gravy is wanted you can add one more cup (160ml) of water.
  2. Before taking out of flame check for the sourness. If you want more can add one more tbs spn of tamrind juice.

Prepartion time: 15 min
Cooking time: 25 to 30 min
Serves: 6 to 8
