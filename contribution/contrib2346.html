Patrode (Alloo Wadi)

Ingredients

25 Colocasia leaves
2 1/2 cups Coconut Gratings
1 cup Raw Rice
A handful Thur Dal
25 roasted Red Chillies
1 small piece Asafoetida
1 lemon sized Tamarind lump
Salt to taste

Method

  1. Remove fibres and stems from colocasia leaves (arvi leaves) and wash thoroughly.
  2. Soak rice and thur dal in water for an hour.
  3. Grind coconut gratings alongwith roasted red chillies, tamarind and asafoetida, without adding much water. Before taking out the masala, add salt and soaked rice and dal and grind to a smooth (but not too fine) paste. It should be dry enough to stick to the leaf surface.
  4. Spread a large arvi leaf on a wooden board with back surface up and stalk end towards you. Smear masala paste evenly all over the surface. Place a smaller leaf over it and repeat the smearing process. Do not pile the leaves one on top of the other, but place it across each other.
  5. After 5-6 leaves are placed one across the other, fold in the edges for about 2" on both sides, smear the folds with some paste and then roll gently but firmly, from the stalk end to the tip, in the shape of a cylinder.
  6. Tie it with a thread if the roll is loose.
  7. Cut each roll crosswise into two pieces, and steam it for about 45 minutes.
  8. Take out and cut into desired thickness (1/2") and serve hot.

Variations

Roasted/Fried/Seasoned Patrodes or Patras

  1. After cutting the rolls into desired thickness, smear dosa pan with oil. When hot, roast the slices on it, till both sides turn brown.
  2. Dust cooked patrode slices with rava, and deep fry them in hot oil.
  3. Take sufficient oil in a frying pan. Prepare seasoning with mustard and curry leaves. Cut each uncooked slice of patrode into 1" thickness. Place the slices in the seasoning side by side. Sprinkle some water over it. Cover it with a lid and cook till browned on one side. Turn all the slices and cook (without covering) it till brown. This is an alternative method of cooking patrode without steaming.
