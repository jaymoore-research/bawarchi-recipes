Cabbage Leaf Pakodas

Ingredients

1 medium sized Cabbage (Green/Purple)
2 medium sized Potatoes boiled and mashed.
1/4 teaspoon Garam Masala Powder
A pinch of Baking Soda
1 cup Garbanzo Bean Flour (besan)
1 teaspoon ground Cumin (jeera powder)
1 teaspoon ground Coriander (dhania powder)
1 teaspoon dried Fenugreek Leaves (methi leaves)
1/2 teaspoon crushed Carom Seeds (ajwain)
1/2 teaspoon Paprika (red chilli powder)
1 teaspoon Salt or to taste
1 tablespoon peeled and finely minced /ground Ginger
1/2 cup Water (or as needed to make the batter slighly thicker than cake batter the flour should stick like small lumps to the leaves)
2 to 3 cups Oil to fry
Sprigs of Cilantro for garnish, a few sprigs chopped (dhania patta)

Method:

  1. First cut the cabbage into two halves and boil it in water in the microwave or on the stove for 5-7 mins. The leaves colour should not change so add a pinch of baking soda while doing this. Be careful not to overboil! The leaves should just wilt and soften. After this separate the leaves and drain out the water.
  2. Sift the besan in a medium size bowl and mix in the jeerapowder, dhania powder, red chilli powder and the salt add water to make a thick paste. (Just before dipping the rolled cabbage leaves into it add hot oil to the batter then pakodas are crisp when fried.)
  3. In the mashed potatoes add the garam masala and a pinch of salt and chopped dhania leaves.
  4. Now carefully take the separate cabbage leaves and put a little amount of mashed potato spread it evenly as possible. Cover with another leaf. Roll the whole thing (like a carpet!).
  5. Cut into smaller pieces if you feel like.
  6. Now dip this into the thick batter. Keep aside. Repeat this for all the leaves.
  7. Heat the oil in the wok /skillet over high heat. (Test if the oil is ready by dropping a pinch of batter in the oil, if it rises to the top immediately the oil is hot and ready for frying) and now fry all the pakodas.

Don't they look colourful!! Have fun cooking!!
