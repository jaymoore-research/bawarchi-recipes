Adai

Ingredients

Bengal gram (channa dhal) - 3/4 cup
Tuar Dhal - 2/3 of a cup
Urad dhal - 1/4 of a cup
Par boiled Rice - 1/2 cup
Raw Rice - 1/2 cup
Green Chilli - 1 big or 2 small
Dry Red chillies - 3 to 4 nos
Hing - a very generous pinch
Salt - to taste
Oil - for shallow frying

For the topping:

Finely chopped Onions
Grated fresh Coconut
Finely chopped dhania leaves
Curry leaves
Butter

Method:

  1. Soak the dhals and the rice separately for 2 hours.
  2. Using a mixie or wet grinder grind the dhals, rice and the green and red chillies coarsely. Mix the hing and salt.
  3. Heat a thick tava (like the one used to make a dosa). Using a flat ladle pour 2 big spoonsful of the batter to make a small pancake maybe of 4" to 5" diameter and atleast 1/2" thick.
  4. Add the onions, coconut, curry leaves and dhania on the top. Make two small holes with a skewer and pour some drops of oil. This is done for even cooking.
  5. Add drops of oil on the outer edge of the adai like the way you do for dosa. Ensure that the heat is moderate and takes a little more time to cook. Now turn over the adai and repeat cooking the other side.
  6. Remove from fire when done and spread some butter on it before serving. Alternatively the toppings can be mixed with the batter and the adais made as described above.

Makes atleast a dozen adais.
