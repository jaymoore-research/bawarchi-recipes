Corn Curry

Corn cobs are roasted on charcoal and rubbed with lemon juice, salt and chilli powder in India. In season, vendors fill the atmosphere with these delicious aromas. Corn is also a popular curry ingredient.

Ingredients

4 whole corn-cobs fresh, canned or frozen
vegetable oil for frying
1 large onion, finely chopped
2 cloves garlic, crushed
1 piece fresh ginger, crushed
/12 teaspoon turmeric
1/2 teaspoon onion seeds
1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
1/2 teaspoon garam masala
chilli powder to taste
6-8 curry leaves
200 ml plain yoghurt
salt to taste

Method:

  1. Cut each corn-cob in half, using a sharp, heavy knife to make clean cuts and prevent damaging the kernels.
  2. Heat the oil in a wok and fry the corn pieces until golden brown on all the sides. Remove the pieces and keep it aside.
  3. Remove any excess oil leaving about 2 tablespoon in the wok.
  4. Grind the onion, garlic and ginger to a paste using a food processor. Next mix all the spices, curry leaves and salt.
  5. Heat the oil gently and fry the onion mixture until all the spices have blended well and oil separates from the masala.
  6. Cool the mixture and gradually fold in the yoghurt. mix well until you have a smooth sauce.
  7. Add the corn to this mixture and mix well so that all the pieces are evenly covered with the gravy.
  8. Gently reheat for about 10 minutes and serve hot.

Recipe secret; In some countries you get yellow colour corn which is little sweet in taste so we add salt to lessen the sweetness. If the corn is not sweet then add 1/2 teaspoon sugar instead of salt to get good taste.

Serves 4-6
