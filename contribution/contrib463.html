Churan-Channa Kozhambu/Kurma

Ingredients

White Kabuli Channa - 1-1/2 (one and half cup) equivalent to 150 gram
Churan - (Saenai in tamil) - 100 gms - sliced medium of 1/2" cubes/dices
Tamarind paste or liquidised - 1 cup
Tomato puree - 15 grms
Tomato medium - 2nos Cut into small pieces
Onion medium - 2 teaspoons (finely cut)
Coconut - scrapped - 6 tea spoons
Green Chillies - 2 Nos
Turmeric powder - 1/4 tea spoon
Red Chilli powder - 1 tea spoon
Masala powder (if needed)
Coriander leaves for garnishing
Oil - one teaspoon
Salt to taste
Muster seeds and Udith dhall for garnishing top

Suitable for 3 persons

Method:

  1. Soak the Channa over night in water pinched with salt.
  2. Drain the water, and steam it on a cooker/rice cooker. And keep aside.
  3. Prepare Tamarind juice from the tamarind paste to 2 cups.
  4. Saute in little oil Scrapped Coconut and Chillies.
  5. Grind the coconut and chillies and make it as paste and keep aside.
  6. Heat little oil in a cooking vessel, add mustard seeds, udath dhal, (masala powder) and onion and saute them.
  7. To the above add salt, tamarind juice, sliced churan and keep them for 5 minutes and boil it till churan is soft.
  8. Add to this liquid turmeric and chilli powder while the above is on.
  9. After that add coconut paste and tomato puree to the above and keep it boiled till the masala fragrance is smelt. This is could happen in 4 to 6 minutes depending on the heat and the vessel.
  10. Finally add to this blended kurma or kozhambu, the already boiled channas and keep it on the heat for 2 mts.
  11. Garnish the top with finely chopped coriander leaves and serve it.

This will go with Hot puris Chappathi, Parathas and with rice also.
