Green/Herb Dosa

Ingredients

2 cups rice
3/4 cup urad dal
1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds
1/4 cup pressed rice/poha
A handful of fennel leaves
A lemon sized ball of jaggery
1-1 1/2 tsp salt or to taste

Method

  1. Wash and soak the rice, dal and methi seeds in enough water to be 2 inches above the dal-rice mixture for 4-5 hours.
  2. Wash and soak the pressed rice/poha in just enough water for 30 mins.
  3. Clean, wash and roughly chop the fennel leaves.
  4. Add pressed rice/poha and chopped fennel leaves to dal-rice-methi.
  5. Grind the above mixture in batches with some water to a fine paste.
  6. The ground dosa batter should be of a thick pouring consistency.
  7. Allow to ferment for 5-6 hours or overnight.
  8. Dissolve jaggery and salt in 1/2 cup water and mix gently but well into batter.
  9. Heat a non-stick pan. Add a tsp of oil.
  10. Spread it around the pan with a piece of cloth or with the cross section of an onion chopped in half.
  11. Pour 11/2 -2 ladles of the batter on the pan and quickly spread it out to desired thickness.
  12. When done on one side flip it over and roast for a minute on the other side.
  13. Repeat till all dosas are done.
  14. Serve with ghee or a sweet and sour/tamarind-date chutney.

N.B. These are very soft and spongy dosas. The fennel gives it a beautiful green colour. The fennel and methi also give this dosa a unique flavour and bitter sweet taste. Add just enough jaggery to give it a slightly sweet taste. A little extra jaggery may cause the dosa to stick to the pan.
