Keema Ghugni

Ingredients

1] One cup of Keema.
2] One cup of Chhola.
3] One big Onion : sliced finely(not chopped).
4] One big Potato : cut into small pieces.
5] One medium Tomato: cut into pieces.
6] 4-5 Green Chillies : sliced lengthwise.
OR

3-4 Dry Red Chillies
7] 1 tbsp. Turmeric powder.
8] 1 1/2 tbsp. Curry powder.
9] 1 tbsp. Coriander powder (dry roasted).
10] 1 tbsp. Cummin powder (dry roasted).
11] 1/4th tbsp. Cinnamon powder.
12] 1 tbsp. Ginger-Garlic paste.
13] 1/2 tbsp. Sugar
14] Salt, Ghee / Mustard oil/ Veg.oil
15] 10-15 Persley leaves.
16] A handful of Curry leaves.
17] 2-3 Bay leaves.

Method:

  1. Pressure cook Chhola, thigh, tomato, potato and persley leaves with the turmeric powder in sufficient water.
  2. Heat oil/ghee in a deep bottomed vessel. Add dry red chillies (if you're using them) along with bay leaves. Or, add bay leaves only. Fry for 2 seconds, and add green chillies and onions.
  3. Bring the thigh piece out from cooker. Wash in cold water. Make pieces. Add to the onions. Deep fry.
  4. Mix curry and cinnamon powder, ginger-garlic paste, sugar and salt in half cup of water.
  5. Add to the fried masala. Stir well.
  6. Let it dry. Drain some water from cooker and add the rest along with other boiled ingredients to the vessel. Add curry leaves.
  7. Let it boil on medium heat until the gravy thickens.
  8. Add dry roasted powders. Let it thicken a little more and remove from heat.

Tips:

  1. There is another way: Boiling the keema separately, and then frying them along with onions, potatoes and green/red chillies. You can then drain away the whole chicken stock if you don't want to add it to the main dish.
  2. The dish prepared in Mustard oil will be more tasty than prepared in Veg.oil/Ghee.
  3. You can avoid curry leaves. People generally don't like chewing leaves!!
