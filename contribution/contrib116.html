Carrot Halwa

Ingredients

to make about 200gms of halwa

20 Carrots (very tender or preferrably baby carrots)
2 cups Milk
1-2 cups sugar
raisins and cashews
ghee

Method

  1. Grate the carrots preferrably using a hand-held grater, if not use a blender.
  2. Soak this grated carrots in milk, make sure it is completely soaked.
  3. Cook the mix in a pressure cooker until you hear 2 whistles.
  4. Once the pressure dies down, make sure the carrots are well cooked, but not mashed.
  5. Now pour 2-3 spoons of ghee in a wide-mouthed vessel (a non-stick one preferrably).
  6. Pour the cooked carrot/milk mix into the vessel.
  7. Add the sugar (about 1-1/2 cups).
  8. Let this mix boil.
  9. Stir constantly (as the mix gets solidified, you can see carrot lava splashing out, so be careful!).
  10. Once it has reached the required level of solidification, fry some raisins and cashews in ghee, in a separate vessel.
  11. Spread the halwa in a plate and decorate the raisins and cashews on the top.
  12. Let it cool. It tastes much better the next day.
