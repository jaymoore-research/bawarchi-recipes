Spicy Rice/Mung Bean Noodles

Ingredients

1. Packet Rice Noodles or Mung Bean Noodles (available in Chinese shops)
2. 1/2 cup fresh/ desiccated coconut
3. 10-12 dry red chillies
4. 1/4 teaspoon mustard seeds for grinding and 1/4 teaspoon for seasoning
5. 1 teaspoon fried gram (gram used for making chutney) (optional)
6. A pinch of aesfoetida
7. Handful of curry leaves
8. Urad dahl for seasoning
9. One lemon size tamarind juice or 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
10. Salt to taste (one to one and a half teaspoon)
11. Jaggery for taste (If not available, 1 tablespoon sugar can be substituted)
12. Coriander for garnishing

Method:

  1. Soak rice noodles or mung bean noodles in hot water until the mixture is ground and seasoned.
  2. Method for Mixture: Coarsely grind coconut, red chillies, mustard, fried gram, tamarind paste/juice and jaggery/sugar to a dry paste consistency with adding a little bit of water.
  3. Now take few tablespoons of oil in a kadai. Heat it. Then season using mustard seeds, aesfoetida, dahl and curry leaves. Can also use groundnuts for seasoning and should be done till golden brown. Once golden brown, put the ground mixture along with salt to taste and saute until it leaves the pan.
  4. Now drain the rice noodles or mung bean noodles and flush it in cold water and keep aside.
  5. Add the sauted mixture to the flushed noodles and mix it thoroughly.
  6. Garnish with coriander leaves.
  7. Can be served hot or cold.
