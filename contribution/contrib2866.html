Vegetables and Rice

Ingredients

Rice:

2 cups Basmati Rice
3.5 cups water
1 tsp. jeera
1 tsp. oil

Method

  1. Wash the rice well.
  2. Heat oil, add jeera, then the rice and fry till rice for 2 min.
  3. Add the water and set flame on high, bring to a boil. After 5 mins of boiling, reduce heat, cover and cook for 10 more min. Rice is done.

Vegetables:

1 large Potato
2 medium Carrots
2 Bell Peppers
3 large Onions
6 plum Tomatoes
1/4 head small Cabbage
1 cup Cauliflower florets
1 cup frozen or fresh Green Peas
1/2 cup Corn
(any other vegetables - like Zucchini, Chow-chow, Red Bell Pepper etc.)
1 tsp. Jeera
1/2 tsp. Mustard
3 tbsp. Oil
3-5 tbsp. Patak's tandoori paste
Salt to taste
1 cup chopped Coriander Leaves

Method

  1. Cut all vegetables into 1" long strips.
  2. Slice onions into very fine long slices.
  3. Heat oil, add the mustard and jeera. Add onions and fry till well browned. Then add all the hard vegetables (carrots, peppers, potatoes, cabbage, cauliflower etc.) Stir and cook for 5 min.
  4. Add the remaining vegetables, except tomatoes. Stir for 3 min.
  5. Add 2 cups water and cover and simmer for 5 min. Check if veg are cooked, then add the salt, tomatoes, tandoori paste and stir well to mix. Simmer till vegetables are very well cooked. (To get a good gravy consistency) -
  6. Take about 2 big ladles of the vegetables and puree in a blender.
  7. Add pureed vegetables to the cooking vegetables.
  8. Garnish with coriander leaves.

(The tandoori paste is slightly sour - due to the ascetic acid or vinegar for preserving) - so if you desire, add a couple of green chillies slit lengthwise, while adding the onions. Or add 1-2 tsp. of red chilli powder to make it more spicy.

For a more nutritious version:

Add soy nuggets soaked in hot water for 10 min. and drained, to the blender when pureeing the vegetables. (this adds protein).
