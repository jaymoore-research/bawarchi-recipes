Mochar Ghonto

Ingredients for Cooking

Mocha is basically flower of Banana.The recipe is bit difficult and time taking but Once its made ,the pain goes after its tasted. Its traditional and enjoyed by Bengalis but will be enjoyed by Non Bengalis who are fond of eating diff varieties of Food.

One Mocha or the Flower of Banana
2 nos of Medium sized Potatoes.
4-5 Green chillies to be slitted
Elaichi,Darchini,Long 1no each for grinding (as Garam Masalas)
1 tbsp turmeric powder
1 tsp salt
1/2 tsp of Sugar for caramalising.
1 tbsp of Ghee.
4-5 tbsp of Mustard Oil.
1/2 Coconut
4 tbsp of Bengal Gram(soaked in water)

To be Grinded:
1 tsp of Jeera
1 tsp of Dhania
1 piece of Ginger.

Methods for Cooking:

  1. Clean the florets of the flower properly, take the middle stem of the florets and cut all the florets into pieces.
  2. Soak the florets pieces in lime water used for beetle leaves (Pan) for more than a hour.
  3. If the lime is not available it can be soaked in turmeric powder and water.
  4. Cut the potatoes into pieces.
  5. Boil the florets in plain water after taken out from the lime water or turmeric water for 10 minutes or boil in the pressure cooker for 3-4 whistles.
  6. Drain out the water and keep it aside.
  7. Heat the skillet or kadai with 4-5 Tablespoon of oil and fry the e potatoes. Keep it aside.
  8. Put 2-3 bay leaves in the rest of remaining oil along with sugar 1/2 tsp for caramalising.
  9. Make fine pieces of coconut and fry it in the oil with 1/2 teaspoon of whole jeera.
  10. Put the soaked bengal grams in it and add the masala paste to it.
  11. Fry all the ingredients and put the fried potatoes in it along with the boiled florets along with 1 tsp of salt and 1 tsp turmeric powder.
  12. Put the flame on slow heat and pour 1/2 cup of water in it to boil the potatoes.
  13. After the potatoes are boiled and the water get dried, pour the garamasala and ghee for taste.
  14. Simmer it for sometime and serve it with Bengali moong daal and rice.
