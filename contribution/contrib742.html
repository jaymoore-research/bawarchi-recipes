Tomato-Coriander Chutney

Ingredients

6-7 medium sized ripe red tomatoes
1 medium bunch of fresh coriander leaves
3 tbsp. cooking oil
5-6 dry red chillies
2 tbsp urad dal
1 tbsp. cooking oil
salt to taste

Method:

  1. Wash and wipe tomatoes.
  2. Clean and cut finely chopped coriander leaves
  3. Take a frying pan and pour 3 tbsp cooking oil and heat it on low flame.
  4. When the oil gets warm, place the tomatoes and close it with a lid and cook on low flame till tomatoes become soft. (Occasionally remove the lid to check that tomatoes do not get burnt or do not stick to the pan.)
  5. After tomatoes are cooked, remove from fire and let them cool.
  6. Take a small size kadhai (pan with a handle for tadka) pour the 1tbsp. oil in it and heat it. Once the oil is heated, add to this 2tbsp. urad dal. When the urad dal becomes pink or golden brown add to it the dry red chillies and fry for 1 minute till the chillies become well coated in oil and turn red. Ensure that dal and chillies do not burn.
  7. Let this tadka mixture cool and then add it to the cooked tomatoes.
  8. Take a mixer and pour in it the tomatoes, fried chillies and urad dal, and the chopped coriander leaves and grind it well with salt (to be added to suit individual taste.) to form a nice smooth paste. Your chutney is ready for you to eat. You can alter the thickness of the paste by adding a little water (at any rate not more than 1/4 - 1/2 cup.)

This goes extremely well with hot chappati, hot rice & roasted papad and alternative chutney for bread sandwiches or even other assortments like pakoras, bhajiyas! So cook it fast and eat and let me know your views at gayatri_kumar@hotmail.com
