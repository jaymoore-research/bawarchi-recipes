Grits Upma

Ingredients

1 cup - Quaker quick Grits
4 cups -ĀWater
1/2- Onion chopped
small piece of ginger
7- Curry leaves
1 tsp -Mustard seeds
1 tsp- Urad dal
1/4 tsp- Turmeric
3 -Green chilies
1 -Medium sized tomato
3 tbsp- Ghee
2 tbsp-ĀDesiccated coconut

Method

  1. Take a pan and dry roast the grits for 1 minute on low flame.
  2. Remove it before it changes the color and keep it aside.
  3. In the sameĀpanĀpour some ghee,add mustard seeds and urad dal.
  4. After the mustard seeds splutter, add curry leaves, onions, ginger, turmeric and green chilies, then fry until the onion becomes light brown.
  5. Add tomato and salt and fry on low flame till the tomato is cooked.
  6. Now pour 4 cups of water and bring it to a boil.
  7. After the water boils,Āadd the grits.
  8. Cover it with a lid and stir occasionally.
  9. Cook the grits onĀlow flame until soft.
  10. Garnish with some desiccated coconut and serve hot.

Useful tips

  1. Unlike rava, it takes few more time for the grits to be cooked, so add the right amount of water to get the desired result.
  2. Just for a change,try making rava idiliĀ,rava dokhlaĀor rava kheer by substitutingĀrava with grits.
  3. While making chapattis,puris,etc, if you don't know the exact quantity of salt while adding directly to the flour, then add the salt first to the water whichĀyou kept aside for kneading.
  4. Taste the water whether the salt is appropriate and then start kneading.
  5. According to the Ayurveda, ghee is better that butter. Ghee is lactose free and promotes all three aspects of mental functioning -- learning, memory and recall.It also enhance digestion if eaten in a right quantity.
  6. To prevent coconut from spoiling,grate the coconut and storeĀit in a freezer.
  7. Use green chilies as a substitute for red chilies, because green chilies are good for health, rather than red chilies.
