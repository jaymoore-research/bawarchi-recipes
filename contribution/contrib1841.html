Spicy Tomato Chutney

Ingredients

4 big tomatoes,
10 pods of garlic
1 small piece of ginger
Tamarind (size of a lemon) soak it in 1/4 cup of water for about 30 minutes.
Salt - 3 tsp
Dry mirchi powder - 4 tsp
Methi seeds - 1tsp
Mustard seeds - 1tsp
Curry leaves
1/2 tsp urad dal,
1/2 tsp jeera
Oil - 1/2 cup

Method:

  1. In a big pan add 2tsp of oil and add the tomaotes, let them get cooked, do not add water but keep stirring them and keep a lid on the pan. After a while, add tamarind pulp which is extracted and salt.
  2. After the tomato pulp gets little thickened without any water in it, cool it for some time and put in in a blender and make it into a fine paste.
  3. Make paste with ginger and garlic and add this to the tomato pulp.
  4. In a dry pan fry the methi and the mustard seeds and make powder of it. Add this and the dry mirch powder to the tomato pulp
  5. In a small pan heat the oil, add some garlic pods, mustard seeds, jeera, urad dal, curry leaves, and once the garlic and urad dal turns golden brown and the mustard seeds splitter add this to the tomato pulp.

This tastes good with rice, or any of the south indian tiffins. Mirch powder can be increased or decreased according to the taste.

Note: This chutney will last for about 2 weeks if refrigirated.
