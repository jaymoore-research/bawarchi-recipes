Chicken Korma

Ingredients

6 chicken drumsticks
6 chicken thighs
2" piece ginger
10 garlic cloves
10-15 green chillies (the short thin thai chili)
2 medium onions
1 1/2 tsp garam masala powder
1/2 tsp clove powder
1/2 tsp cinammon powder
1 1/2 tsp freshly ground black peppercorns
1/2 cup vinegar
1 tsp soy sauce
1/2 cup finely chopped coriander leaves
3 medium tomatoes, chopped
Finely chopped coriander leaves for garnish
Salt to taste
2 tbsp cooking oil

Method

  1. Blend together the onions, ginger, garlic and green chillies.
  2. Mix together the garam masala powder, clove powder, cinammon powder, ground peppercorns, vinegar, salt and soy sauce. This is the marinade.
  3. Cut the chicken thighs into small pieces and pierce the chicken drumsticks and thighs with a fork. This helps the marination process.
  4. In a deep bowl, mix together the chicken and the marinade and keep aside for about 20 mins.
  5. In a saucepan, heat some cooking oil and add the ginger/garlic/onion/chilli paste.
  6. Fry the paste, constantly stirring it and when it turns brown in color and the oil starts leaving the sides of the paste, add the marinated chicken, chopped coriander and tomato and about 1 cup of water.
  7. Simmer the curry on medium heat for about 20 mins until the chicken is cooked. Check for salt and add more if needed.
  8. Serve hot with steamed rice.
  9. Since this dish gets it's heat from green chillies, peppercorns, cloves and cinammon, adjust the above quantities to taste.
  10. You can also add roasted cashewnut paste to the curry just before you take it off the stovetop, for a richer gravy.

Happy cooking!
