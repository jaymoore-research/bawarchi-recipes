Polli

Ingredients

For the Dough

Maida (Plain Flour)-2 1/4 cups
Oil (preferably gingelly oil) -1/2 cup
Turmeric Powder-1/2 teaspoon
Salt -1/2 teaspoon
Ghee to spread over the Pollis

For the Filling

Bengalgram Dal-1 1/2 cups
Redgram Dal -1/2 cup
Coconut -1, grated
Jaggery -1 1/2 Kg, powdered
Cardamoms -4 to 6, powdered

Method

  1. Make a dough with the flour, salt, turmeric powder, 1/4 cup of the oil and sufficient water (it should resemble a chapathi dough).
  2. Pour the remaining oil on the dough. Set aside.

To make the filling:

  1. Pressure cook the dals. Drain & set aside. Make sure they are not overcooked.
  2. Roast the grated coconut for a couple of minutes. Add the powdered jaggery & simmer on a low heat till the jaggery melts completely.
  3. Add the cooked dals & continue to simmer till the mixture blends & thickens. Set aside the filling & allow it to cool.
  4. In a liquidiser grind the cooled filling into a fine paste, adding the powdered cardamoms.
  5. Mix together the oil & the dough.
  6. Take a small ball of the dough and using a rolling pin flatten them into round chapathis.
  7. Take a small lump of the filling & place it on the centre of the flattened dough. Fold the dough over the filling once again (as U would do for Aloo Paratha etc.)
  8. Heat the tawa and fry both sides till done, adding very little oil.
  9. Serve hot/cold with ghee spread over the Polli.

Note: These delicious Pollis can be stored in an airtight container/ziplocks covers for a couple of days. They can also be refrigerated.

Makes : 20-25 Pollis.
