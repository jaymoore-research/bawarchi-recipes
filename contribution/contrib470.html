Hi! I'm Dipa, and I'd like to share with you a very, very old recipe called 'Lasan Ni Chutney'. Its an extremely versatile chutney, which you can use in cooking, to mixing it with yogurt (to make a Garlic 'Raita'), to spreading it over cubes of paneer and grilling on the barbeque!
You can email me on: DValambhia@aol.com should you have any other uses for it.

Lasan (Garlic) Ni Chutney

Ingredients

20 cloves Fresh Garlic
2 teaspoons of Chillie powder (or according to taste)
1/2 teaspoon of salt (or according to taste)
2 teaspoons of vegetable oil
Method

  1. Peel and grind the garlic until it is a paste.
  2. Add the chillie power, salt and oil to the garlic.
  3. Mix again thoroughly.
  4. You should have a thick, but smooth paste (a bit like toothpaste, but don't brush your teeth with this!)
  5. Enjoy the chutney in a variety of ways! Innovation is the answer!
Storage

The chutney should be stored in an airtight container in the fridge or a cool, dark place. If stored in the fridge, it will last as long as it is not eaten up!

Uses

  • Mix a little chutney with some yogurt and use as a dip.
  • Add a little more oil to the chutney so it is more 'loose' in consistency, to marinade paneer, potatoes, mushrooms and other vegetables for a barbecue.
  • The chutney tastes great when eaten with hot rotis, rotlas, parantha, puris, bhakharis, naan etc etc.
  • The chutney can also be used in cooking, where chillie, garlic and salt are required (just remember to omit the chillie, salt and garlic the recipe asks for!)
