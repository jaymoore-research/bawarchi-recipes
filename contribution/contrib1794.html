(Moor in Tamil means buttermilk and Kulu implies a thing whose consistency is semi-solid).

MoorKulu

Ingredients

Two cups of fresh Buttermilk (Churn 1 cup Curds along with 1 cup Water to get 2 cups of Buttermilk)
3/4 cup Rice Flour (Flour of fine consistency)
1 TableSpoon Oil

Seasoning:

1 teaspoon Mustard seeds
2 Chillies finely chopped
25 gms Ginger finely cut

Garnishing:

A few Curry Leaves, Coriander Leaves finely chopped
Fresh Lime Juice 1 tablespoon
Salt to taste

Method:

  1. First put the rice flour in a large bowl, pour the buttermilk little by little and constantly keep mixing till a liquid consistency of the mixture is obtained. If buttermilk is insufficient, add water to obtain a liquid like consistency.
  2. Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, after it splutters put the chopped chillies, ginger pieces and curry leaves.
  3. Keep the stove on a low flame and pour the rice/buttermilk mixture.
  4. Add salt to taste. Keep stirring constantly till a semisolid consistency is obtained. Do not allow the mixture to become thick. Remove from flame and garnish with coriander leaves.
  5. Add a few drops of lemon juice just before serving.

This dish is best eaten when hot.
