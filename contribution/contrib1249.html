Mutton Masaledar

Ingredients

1 1/2 pounds of Mutton , washed well and cut into 2" pieces
3-4 large Potatoes, peeled and cut into 4 pieces each (if the pieces are smaller, they will disintegrate while pressure cooking!)
2 large Onions, finely chopped
1 large Tomato, finely chopped
2-3 spoons Ginger-Garlic paste
2 Red Chillies
3-4 Green Chillies, slit
1 spoon each Jeera, Dhaniya powder
A pinch of Garam masala
Half a spoon Red Chili powder
Pinch of Turmeric
2-3 Cloves
1" Cinnamon stick
2-3 Cardamon pods
2 Bay leaves
2 spoons of Curd.

Method :

  1. Marinate for an hour or so the mutton pieces with the onions (2/3 of all the onions cut), tomato, green chillies, ginger -garlic paste, 1 bay leaf, the whole garam masalas, curd and the masala powders (not the garam masala powder.) Do not add salt at this point.
  2. Fry the potato pieces lightly with some salt and turmeric and keep aside.
  3. To the skillet add some oil, add the bay leaf and the red chili and roast till red.
  4. Then add the rest of the onions and fry till golden brown.
  5. Add the mutton pieces along with the marinade and salt and keep stirring and frying on medium-low heat till the mutton pieces are fried a bit and you can see the oil separating. (it should take you atelast 30 minutes to get to this stage).
  6. Add the fried potato pieces and stir some more.
  7. Add hot boiling water (quantity depends upon the amount of gravy you want; I suggest that you do not add too much water).
  8. Pressure cook upto 3 whistles. Turn off the heat.
  9. Carefully uncover the pressure cooker, add the garam masala, stir once and cover again.
  10. Serve hot after 5-10 minutes (to let the flavors settle).

This mutton curry is yum with either rice/rotis, lemon slices and an onion salad.
