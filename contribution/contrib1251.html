Mutton/Chicken Curry

Ingredients

Chicken/Mutton - 1 kg (chopped into medium sizes)
Coconut - 1/2 (Large size, grated)
Garlic - 8-10 medium sized flakes
Ginger - 1 inch piece (medium thickness)
Onion - 2 sliced (medium size)
Somph - 2 tsp
Khus-Khus - 2 tsp
Chilli powder - 3 tsp
Coriander powder - 6 tsp
Curry leaves - few
Mustard - 1 tsp
Cooking oil - 3 tbsp (add more, depending on u'r liking)
Salt to taste

Method:

  1. Blend garlic and ginger to a paste
  2. Make a fine paste of coconut, somph and khus-khus
  3. Heat oil in a thick-bottomed vessel. Saute mustard, onion and curry leaves.
  4. Keep flame in sim. Add ginger-garlic paste and stir until raw smell disappears
  5. Add chilli, coriander powder and stir until oil separates.
  6. Add coconut paste. Continue to stir until oil separates.
  7. Finally, add the chicken or mutton pieces. Stir once and add water. (You can clear the paste contents in the mixer jars with some water and use the same) Note : Water content should be a little more than the desired consistency.
  8. Add salt. Increase the flame and once it starts boiling, the flame can be reduced and maintained until the desired consistency is obtained.
  9. Stir in between.

Chicken curry and idli make a very good combination.

Note : For mutton, it is advisable to use a cooker instead of an ordinary vessel, as mutton requires more time to get cooked well. If u use a cooker, after cooking u'll have to transfer the contents to another vessel and keep for a few minutes in SIM.
