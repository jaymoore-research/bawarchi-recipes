Prawn Malaikaari

Ingredients

Prawn (1 tray of shelled and cleaned medium sized Florida shrimps; outside of U.S., buy by the weight, and thoroughly clean and de-vein the prawns. Buy preferably a little larger size)
Potatoes (2 meduim or large; diced into pieces)
Onions (2 large)
Garlic (1-2 teaspoons of garlic paste or 3 cloves of garlic chopped into very fine pieces)
Ginger (1-2 teaspoons of ginger paste or 1/2 inch ginger root blended in the mixer)
Green Chillies or Red Chilly Powder (as much as you like)
Coconut Milk (1/2 a can) (You can get it at any Indian or South East Asian grocery store. If you are using a fresh coconut, squeeze the grated coconut flakes for the milk, and also keep the flakes)
Coconut Powder or Flakes (4-5 teaspoons) (If you have used a fresh coconut, then you can use the remaining flakes instead of the powder)
Garam Masala (2-3 teaspoons)
Turmeric Powder (1 teaspoon, for color)
Cinnamon Sticks (1-2 broken)
Cardammom or Elaichi (about 4-5; crush into powder in the blender)
Canola Oil (3 tablespoons) (You can use more if you want. I prefer Canola oil since it has the lowest amount of saturated fat, and thus is comparatively more healthy).

Method :

  1. Clean the prawns/shrimps and keep aside (if you are using the ready-to-cook Florida shrimps). Or else, clean and de-vein the prawns properly.
  2. Heat oil in a wide pan or Wok. Stir fry the ginger paste, the garlic paste, the chopped onions, the cinnamon sticks, and the elaichi for several minutes (5-7 minutes) in medium-low to medium heat.
  3. Then add the turmeric, the shrimps, and the coconut flakes. Stir fry in medium-low heat till the shrimps/prawns begin to turn reddish (3-4 minutes more).
  4. Then add the potatoes, the garam masaala, and the coconut milk; continue stirring. Then add about a half cup to a full cup of water. The amount of water would depend on your taste. Add as much chilly powder as you would want.
  5. Cover up and cook for about 30-35 minutes. Cooking this dish in medium heat, and keeping it covered with a lid throughout, would give the best results.
  6. When covered, the aroma of the spices and the ingredients is trapped the most. Cook till the prawns are cooked and the curry has a thick consistency.
  7. Serve hot with basmati rice or any fine rice.
