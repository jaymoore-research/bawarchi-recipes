This tasty Middle East appetizer (hummus) is made with Madras curry powder without the traditional high fat tahini (sesame paste). It goes well with Indian flatbreads, pita bread, or crackers.

Chick Pea Dip with Indian Spices

Ingredients

1 tsp. Madras Curry Powder
1/2 tsp. Ginger Powder
1 tsp. Cumin Powder
1 tbs. extra virgin Olive Oil
1/4 cup chopped Green Onions (whites and some green)
4 cloves fresh Garlic, chopped
1 tbs. Sesame seeds
1 1/2 can Garbanzo Beans (chick peas), reserve some liquid
1/2 cup or more non fat Yogurt
2 tbs. fresh Lemon Juice
1 tbs. Parsley
Salt and Pepper to taste
Cayenne Pepper or Paprika
Extra Virgin Olive Oil

Method

  1. Toast the spices in a teflon or iron skillet briefly.
  2. Add the oil, onions, garlic and sesame seeds. Saute until the onions are done.
  3. Puree the mixture in a blender with the yogurt, chickpeas, lemon juice and parsley. Add some of the reserved chickpea liquid the chickpea mixture is too thick.
  4. Chill and garnish with a little olive oil and sprinkle with cayenne pepper or paprika before serving.

Serves 6-8 as an appetizer.
