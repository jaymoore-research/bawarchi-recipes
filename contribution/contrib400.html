I am Sunil Patankar. I am originally from Maharashtra, currently employed in Singapore. I would like to propose recipe for preparing "Jhoonka". "Jhoonka" is very popular in rural Maharashtra. Mostly, enjoyed by poor hardworking farming community, it tastes great. Jhoonka goes well with Makka ki Roti. Needless to mention it tastes equally well when had with ordinary roti.

Jhoonka

Ingredients

Besan (gram flour) 100 grams
Tomatoes one medium size
Onion two medium size
Garlic 6-7 cloves
Ginger about 10-15 grams
Salt to taste
Coriander leaves
Cummin seeds (jeera) half tea spoon
Mustard seed half tea spoon
Hing powder

Method

  1. Cut tomato into bits and mix it with besan and chopped coriander leaves and water and salt.
  2. Make sure to use minimum quantity of water.
  3. Then place a dry frying pan on the hotplate.
  4. When the pan is hot enough add to it three table spoons of vegetable oil.
  5. Later add half table spoon of cummin seed followed by mustard seed in the pan.
  6. When the mustard seed begin to crack add to it paste of garlic and ginger. After stirring the paste well in oil add finely chopped onion to the pan.
  7. Fry the pan till it turns light brown.
  8. Then pour the mixture of besan and tomato containing salt and chopped coriander leaves into the pan.
  9. Continuously stir the content in the pan to till the the whole thing becomes almost dry and turns light brown.

And "Jhoonka" is ready to be served.
