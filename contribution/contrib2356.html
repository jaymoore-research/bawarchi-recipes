Vada Curry

Ingredients

Channa Dal - 2 Cups
Onions - 2 Medium
Green Chillies - 4
Garlic - 3-4 pods
Ginger garlic paste - 2 tablespoon
Tomato - 1 big
Red Chilli powder - 1 tablespoon
Mint - a small bunch
Coriander leaves - a small bunch
Saunf (fennel seeds)- 3 tablespoon
Cinnamon,bay leaves - a little
Salt - to taste

Method

  1. Soak Channadal for 8-10 hours and grind it coarsely, add little salt and make small vadas (don't bother about the shape of the vadas) and keep aside.
  2. Next in a broad saucepan add little oil, first add the cardamom and bayleaves. Next add the garlic which is cut lengthwise.
  3. Add the onions (cut lengthwise) and fry till it is brown and add the green chillies slit lengthwise.
  4. Now add gingergarlic paste and fry till the raw smell goes.
  5. After this is done add the chopped tomatoes and red chilli powder, fry for 2 mins and close the lid for few mins till the tomatoes are cooked.
  6. Now add 4-5 cups of water and let it boil. While the water starts boiling add the chopped mint leaves and coriander leaves
  7. Take the vadas and smash it rougly with your hand and add it to the boiling water
  8. Finally add the Fennel seeds which also should be grinded coarsely. Add salt and remove from fire. If the curry becomes very thick add some more water, the curry should ne neither too thick nor too liquid. Now your curry is ready to be served.

P.S. : Don't forget to smash and add the vadas, it should be never added as whole.
