Aloo Bhath

Ingredients

1) Potatos - 4 large
2) Onions - 2 large
3) Cocunut - 1/2
4) Red chilli powder, garam masala, 1/2 spoon Dhania powder, pinch of termeric, ginger garlic paste(1 spoon).
5) Cloves, cinnamon(optional)
6) Basmathi Rice 2 cups
7) Oil 3 tablespoons

  1. Wash Basmathi rice (and soak it for 15 min).
  2. Cut potato into big pieces.
  3. Cut onions into thin slices and fry them in 1/2 spoon of oil.
  4. In a blender grind fried onoins, cocunut and all item (4) ingredients to make it a thick paste. (if you have enough time mix potato and this paste and leave it for an hour to marinate potatos. If not, never mind)
  5. In a pressure pan or a deep sauce take oil. When it gets heated add the paste and potatos and fry it for 3 min.
  6. Add rice and 4 cups water and let it cook until the potatos are tender and rice is cooked.

Serve hot with raitha and/or papad.
When you want to try this with kids make it less spicy and add some ghee.
