Chammandi Podi

Ingredients

1) A large bunch of fresh coriander leaves, stalks removed, chopped and air-dried overnight by spreading on a cloth or newspaper
2) 2 tbsp white til ( sesame) seeds;
3) 4 tbsp chana dall;
4) 4 tbsp fresh grated coconut or dessicated powder;
5) 8 or 10 dried red chillies, depending on how hot you want;
6) 4 cloves garlic, chopped roughly;
7) 1 tbsp tamarind powder/ amchur or a lemon size dry tamarind;
8) 1-1/2 tsp salt, more if needed to your taste, your BP permitting!

Method

  1. Roast dry the sesame seeds till they are about to splutter, add chana dal till it starts turning slightly brown;
  2. Add the dried red chillies, the grated coconut or dessicated powder, and garlic. Roast till the coconut is dry and turns slightly brown ;
  3. Remove and transfer the roasted ingredients to a flat dish to cool
  4. Slightly roast the air- dried coriander leaves
  5. First grind roughly the roasted ingredients along with tamarind/amchur powder and salt, then add the dry roasted coriander leaves and salt and grind some more till all the ingredients are thoroughly mixed to a a consistency not too fine.
  6. Transfer the Chammandi podi to a glass jar;store in the fridge only if you are too squeamish to polish off the lot in two or three days

Optionally substitute fresh mint leaves for coriander leaves or use both in proportions you like best.I have even tried kadi patha in small quantity along with coriander and mint leaves and found the Podi still heavenly with curd rice, idli, dosa, even just as a supplement to any dish to add zip to it.
