Chicken in Jiffy

Ingredients

1 cup - Chicken breast meat sliced into strips, about 3 x 1 x 1 cm
2 Tblsp - Oil
2 pieces - Cinnamon sticks, about 2 x 1 x 1 cm long
4 -6 Whole Cloves
2 tsp - Ginger/Garlic paste (equal amount of fresh ginger and garlic ground or grated)
1/2 cup - Onions, chopped fine
1/2 to 1 tsp - Finely chopped Green Chillies (finger hot peppers or jalapino ) adjust to your taste
1/8 tsp - Turmeric powder
1/2 tsp - Salt (adjust to your taste)
1/2 tsp - Lime or Lemon Juice
4 tbsp - Whipping Cream or Half and Half (or any thick milk in areas where cream is not available)
1/2 cup - Thick Coconut Milk (canned or fresh)
2 tbsp - White Wine
1/2 cup - Chopped fresh Coriander leaves (cilantro)

Method:

  1. Heat oil in a non-stick pot over medium flame. The oil should get quite hot.
  2. Add cinnamon sticks and cloves. Beware! Cloves pop in hot oil. Use a lid to avoid being splattered with hot oil. When the cloves puff up add chopped onions and green chillies. Saute until soft.
  3. Add ginger/garlic paste and roast the mix brown. Extra oil or butter may be added if necessary. Butter adds to flavour and taste.
  4. Add salt, turmeric powder and chicken strips and give a quick stir until the chicken turns white - perhaps for 2 or 3 minutes.
  5. Add lime juice and stir.
  6. Next add cream, coconut milk and wine. Turn the heat up and cook the contents for 4 to 5 more minutes or until the chicken is done. Caution! Do not overcook! Taste and adjust salt. Remove from heat, add chopped coriander leaves and cover the lid. Let the flavours blend before serving.

This can be served with Pulav or Plain Rice.

Cook's note:

  1. Cream blends wonderfully with the flavour of green chillies, coriander leaves and wine.
  2. If coconut milk is not available, cream can be used alone.
  3. The sauce should be creamy and thick. If it is too thin, remove chicken pieces, reduce the sauce and then return the chicken to the pot.
  4. Ginger can be omitted if not available.
  5. Cinnamon and cloves can be replaced by 1/2 tsp Fennel seeds for a different flavour.
  6. Same recipe can be used for mushrooms.
