Hara Pyaz Aaloo / Scallion and Potato

This recipe can be made with raw or boiled potatoes. Lot of fine cut scallion gives it color and flavor. This dry potato vegetable tastes good with plain paratha, puri, kadhi, rajma, lobia or almost anything.

Ingredients:
2 medium potato
2 bunch scallion (about 12)
2 tablespoon cooking oil
1/4 teaspoon haldi / turmeric powder
1/4 teaspoon mirch / chili powder
2 teaspoon dhania / coriander powder
3/4 teaspoon salt
3/4 teaspoon mango powder
1/4 teaspoon garam masala

Method:

  1. Remove outer layer of scallion / bad portions and cut away the root end, and wash.
  2. Make a stack of handful of scallions on a chopping board, hold them with one hand and cut with the other into about 1/8" long pieces (both white & green parts) and set aside.
  3. Either use boiled potatoes (boil potatoes in their skin, peel) or use raw peeled potatoes, cut into about 3/4-1" pieces.
  4. Heat oil in wok or non stick frying pan. Add cut scallion and let it fry for 5-10 minutes - till they shrink and gets a little brown. Add cut potatoes. Add haldi, mirch, dhania, salt and mix If using raw potatoes - reduce the heat and cover, let it cook in steam till done, mixing in between. If using boiled potatoes - reduce the heat a little but do not cover.
  5. Add mango powder and garam masala, mix. Spread the vegetables in the pan and let the masala / spices and vegetables roast to golden (about 5 - 7 minutes). Transfer in a serving dish. Serve with daal, rice or bread. Serves 2-3.
