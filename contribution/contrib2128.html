Moong Dal Dosa

Ingredients

1 cup yellow split Moong Dal
1 cup Raw Rice
2 Green Chillies
1 Onion
25 gms Ginger
50 gms grated Coconut
Finely Chopped Coriander to garnish
Salt to taste
Oil to prepare the dosa

Method

  1. Soak rice and Dal separately for 6 hrs.
  2. Grind the dal and rice separately to a medium consistency.
  3. Now, grind the ginger, chillies, onion and coconut to a fine consistency and mix it well with the batter. Take care to see that the batter does not become too watery.
  4. Finally add salt and chopped coriander leaves.

Use either a nonstick or a conventional tava to prepare the dosa. Each dosa (1 lardel @75gms of batter) can be prepared with 1/2 teaspoon oil on a conventional tava. The batter can stored in fridge for a day. For best results use the batter immediately.

Tip

If conventional tava is used, to make good dosas, clean the tava properly and evenly smear it with one teaspoon of oil and allow it to cure for one hour. Heat the tava on a high flame, once heated bring it to medium flame before pouring the dosa. Before pouring each dosa, use one portion of an onion which has been cut into two pieces, to rub the tava. Each time dip the onion piece in salt water and rub it over the tava. This ensures that the dosas are cooked brown and do not stick to the surface. Yes! I am sure many of the bawarchi readers will agree that dosa on a conventional tava certainly tastes much better than on a non stick one.
