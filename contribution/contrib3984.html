This is a prasada. which will be prepared on the day of "Ugadi", which is also "New year" day for the people of Mysore, which is in south Indian state of Karnataka, India. On that day main doors will be decorated with tender mango leaves with a bunch of flowering neem shoots stuck at the two corners. After the pooja, a mixture of neem leaves and jaggery, is offered to all - the neem, extremely bitter in taste, and jaggery sweet and delicious, signify the two conflicting aspects of human life - joy and sorrow. symbolically representing the fact that life is a mixture both of the sweet as well as the bitter, and one should learn to take both in ones stride. And also Holige or Obbittu (My earlier contribution to bawarchi) a sweet stuffed tortilla-like bread is prepared on this day. They will also share this bevubella with neighbours after seeing the moon. For other mysore recipes visit my website at http://geocities.com/popndoll. Feedback to : popndoll@yahoo.com

Bevu Bella (Ugadi Special)

Ingredients

Bevu leaves (Neem Leaves) 1/4 cup
Bella ( Jaggery)Grated 1/4 cup
Banana sliced 1/4 cup
Honey 2 tsp
Ghee 4 tsp
Coconut 1/4 cup

Method:

Add all the ingredients. Mix well and eat, sharing with every one. You should not chew, you should swallow.

Evening Bevu Bella

Ingredients:

Besan or gram flour 1/2 cup
Jaggery powder 1/2 cup
Coconut 1/2 cup
Neem Leaves 1/4 cup
Cardamom powder 2 tsp

Method:

Powder all the ingredients and serve 2 to 3 spoons to every one. It will be shared with everyone after seeing the moon on "ugadi".
