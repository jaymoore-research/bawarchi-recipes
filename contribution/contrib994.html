Tomato Khadi

Ingredients

1 kg Tomatoes (you can use tinned tomatoes as well, especially Italian plum which gives it extra flavour)
100 gms besan flour
8 pieces Okra /Bhendi slit lengthwise
1 medium sized potato peeled and cubed
100 gms French beans (chopped to about 1 inch)
100 gms baby corn (chopped in half)
1 medium brinjal cubed
Handful of coriander leaves, finely chopped
1 inch root ginger
4 green chillies
2 tbsp oil
2 tsp turmeric
1 tsp red chilli powder
Half a tsp methi seeds
Half a tsp jeera
Salt to taste

Method:

  1. Fry okra and brinjal in oil, drain, mix in a bit of salt and keep aside.
  2. Put tomatoes in boiling water for a minute and peel the skin. Grind it along with green chillies and ginger to a smooth paste. (If using tinned tomatoes, just grind directly).
  3. Heat oil and season with jeera and methi seeds. Add besan and fry it till you get an aroma and till it is light brown in colour.
  4. Add half a litre water, tumeric, chilli powder and all the vegetables, a bit of Jaggery or brown sugar and cook till vegetables are tender though not pulpy. (An easier method is to just steam the vegetables in a pressure cooker and add at this stage.
  5. Add the ground tomato paste, salt and boil.
  6. Add fried brinjal and okra and let it boil for 2/3 minutes.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves just before serving.

(This dish goes extremely well with jeera rice )
