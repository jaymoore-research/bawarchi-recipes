Chicken in Peanut Curry

Ingredients

1 whole Chicken (cleaned and chopped into medium size pieces).
1 tub of Curd (about 2 1/2 cups).
1 cup roasted and skinned Peanuts.
3 medium size Onions.
1 cup chopped Coriander.
A large piece of Ginger.
6 to 7 Green Chillies.
Salt and Pepper to taste.

Method:

  1. Clean and chop the chicken. Marinade it for atleast 10 hours in a mixture of curd, salt and black pepper.
  2. Make a paste of the onions, green chillies, coriander and ginger.
  3. Grind the peanuts into a paste seperately.
  4. Saute the green paste in butter (could replace it with oil, if desired). When the paste begins to turn golden, add the peanut paste and one tablespoon of the curd from the marinade. Stir well to make a thick curry. Now add the chicken pieces, carefully wiping away all excess curd. Cover and cook on medium heat. It will not take more than 15 minutes for the chicken to cook.
  5. When the chicken is done, add the remaining curd from the marinade, bring to a quick boil and take off heat.
  6. Garnish with some coriander and slivers of ginger. Serve hot with kulchas.

Tip: The dish becomes rather rich because of the natural oil in the peanut paste. So use very little butter when you suate the onions.
