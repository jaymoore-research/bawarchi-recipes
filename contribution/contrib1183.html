Swadist Koftae

Ingredients

1 Small Cabbage.
1/2 Cup Besan (Gram Flour).
1 Tspn Salt (swad anusaar).
1/2 Tspn Turmeric Powder.
1/2 Tspn Chilli Powder (Red).
1/2 Tspn Garam Masala Powder.
1/2 Tspn any mixed curry powder.

Method :

  1. Firstly boil the shredded cabbage till its tender. Strain out and keep it to cool in a salad bowl. (Don't throw away the boiled liquid, you can use it later for curry as it has all the nutritive value/vitamins.
  2. Now, after a few minutes put all the other ingredients into the same bowl mentioned above and mix into a dough of flour & cabbage slices.
  3. Heat oil in a deep frying pan and let dumplings of little koftaas form as you dump small balls from the mixture (cabbage slices + flour) you made earlier.
  4. Fry till golden brown and set aside in a plate or brown paper.

Curry

For Curry and gravy (we need....)

1 Onion
2 Green Chillies (chopped)
1 Big Tomato (chopped)
1 1/2 tbsp paste of Ginger & Garlic paste.
Coriander leaves (if desired ) or for garnish.
2 tspn Salt (to taste)
1 tspn Turmeric powder
1 tspn Coriander powder
1 tspn Garam masala powder.
1 tspn Red Chilli powder
1/2 tspn any Curry powder
1 Bay leaf

Method :

  1. Firstly allow the kadai to get heated then put the oil as it starts to change color, put onion and a bay leaf.
  2. Let it caramelize or turn transparent (here we are talking about red onions).
  3. Add ginger + Garlic paste and stir.
  4. After a few seconds add up the chillies and rest of the masala's in a fraction of 2 to 3 seconds (one by one). Keep stirring so that the ingredients doesn't get burnt (add in between little water to avoid burning), as the oil starts getting separated add the tomato and keep stirring so that it gets purried, add water & salt and let the gravy thicken up on its own.
  5. Add pakoras or koftaas to this curry and stir for the koftaas to soften up.
  6. After one boil or two take away from oven.

"Swadist Koftaas are ready for your dinner. Serve in a bowl garnished with coriander leaves on top along with purees or paranthas or plain rice, they will taste excellent.
