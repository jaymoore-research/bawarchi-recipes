This is Rashmi Kulkarni from Fort Worth, Texas. This time, I'll be contributing with Sweet Sheera, a traditional Maharashtrian dish. It is usually served as a "prasad" for Satyanarayan puja or can be made during any auspicious time. It can be also served as a breakfast or a snack. It takes only 15-20 minutes to cook. Sweet Sheera

Ingredients

1 cup of Rava
1 cup of Sugar
1 cup of Milk
1.5 cups of Water
1 gm of crushed Cardamom powder
Dry fruits, if required (Almonds, Cashews)
A pinch of Saffron
1 crushed Banana
2 spoonful of Pure Ghee to fry the rava

Method:

  1. Take the mentioned amount of rava in a deep vessel. Roast it continuously in the vessel, without burning it. Continue doing it for 2-3 minutes on a low flame.
  2. Now, add the mentioned amount of pure ghee, again roast it for sometime (3-4 minutes) on a medium flame without burning the rava. Sprinkle some water over the rava, so that it absorbs water and increases in size. Stir it one more time. The roasted rava can now be removed and placed aside in a dish.
  3. Take the mentioned amount of sugar, milk & water in the same vessel. Add the cardamom powder. Stir the mixture very well. Dry fruits, saffron & banana can be added at this moment. Bring the mixture to a boil & do not forget to lower the flame.
  4. Its time to add the roasted rava, stir it quickly before the lumps are formed. Cover the vessel with a lid, so that the rava gets steam cooked & the liquid evaporates to form a solid mass.
  5. Serve it hot alongwith Kanda Bhaji (Onion Pakora) or Potato Vada.
