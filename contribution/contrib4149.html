Kalyana Payasam

Ingredients

1. Chana dal( kadaliparuppu) 200 grams
2. Moong dal 400 grams
3.Jaggery 500 grams
4. Milk 1 litre
5. Sweetened Condensed Milk one tin (200 grams)
6. Dessicated Coconut about 100 grams.

For dressing:

Cashew 50 grams (split into two)
Badam 10 /15 (peeled and cut itno thin strps)
Dry Grapes 25 grams
Elaichi (cardamom) and a few strands of kesar (kungumappu) Dry Coconut pieces (1/2 cm) if you like it.

Method:

  1. Clean and soak the dals for about 1 hour and then boil them in a pressure cooker and keep aside (keep just sufficent water to boil the dals)
  2. In a very thick bottmed vessel boil the milk and reduce it to 2/3rd of its quantity KEEP Aside.
  3. Powder the jagerry dilute in hot water (1/2 litre) & drain so that the impuries are removed. Boil the jaggery nicely and Keep aside.
  4. To the pre boiled milk add the cooked dal slowly little at a time time so that the dal does not turn into lumps. Keep the gas at simmer and keep stiring continously. Add one half of the dessicated coconut and the condensed milk (one forth of the tin). Simmer for about 15 minutes.
  5. Finally add the jagerry and keep stiring so that the whole mixture attains a uniform consistency. Add the rest of the dessicated coconut and finally simmer for about 5 minutes. (ENSURE THAT YOUR HUSBAND IS NEAR BY SO THAT HE CAN DO THE LABORIOUS STIRRING). If you feel that the payasam is very thick you can add (pre boiled) milk to your requirement.
  6. Dressing: roast in ghee the cashew, badam and kismiss (dry grapes) and finally add the kesar dissolved in milk and toss it up with elaichi powder.

Serves: Eight - Ten adults
Time required A clean one hour.

This was a super hit in one of the pongal dinners of tamil sangam dar es salaam.
