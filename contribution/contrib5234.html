Vazhapoo Porial

Ingredients

Plantain flower - 1
Toor dhal - 50 gm
Spanish red onion - 1medium
Dried red chillies -3 or 4
Cumin seeds - 1/2 table spoon
Turmeric powder 1/8 table spoon
Salt to taste

For seasoning: oil, mustard seeds, urad dhal and curry leaves

Method:

Preparation:

  1. Clean the plantain flower, by removing the outer sheath and the central part. Cut in small pieces. While cleaning, try not to separate each florette, then it will be easy to cut them. After cutting and cleaning immediately immerse them in cold water to avoid browning.
  2. Drain the water from the cut flower and then mix it with the toor dhal and keep it in the fridge for a few hours, preferably over night.
  3. By soaking with dhal, the nutrients are retained at the same time, the bitterness is also gone.

Cooking:

  1. Heat oil in a non stick pan or pressure pan. Add mustard seeds, when it sputters add the urad dhal,dried chillies and then curry leaves.
  2. Add the plantain flower along with the soaked toor dhal, without adding water. (Even the water found at the bottom of the dhal flower mixture can be strained and added if necessary. )
  3. Add the cut onions,cumin seeds,turmeric and salt also.
  4. Close with a lid and cook till done stirring in between. Sprinkle the strained water if needed.
  5. Since the dhal is soaked it'll be cooked soon so no need to pressure cook. Keep an eye and don't let it over cook.
  6. When it's 3/4th done, remove the lid and let all the water evaporate.
  7. Can be served with rice and vatha kulambu.

Note: to avoid the stains in hands while cleaning plantain flower apply coconut or olive oil very well in your hands before doing.
