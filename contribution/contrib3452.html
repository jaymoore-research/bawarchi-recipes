Kutti Vengaaya Kaara Kuzhambu

Ingredients

Pearl Onions - 1 cup
Roma Tomatoes - 2 or Canned Crushed Tomatoes - 1/2 cup
Tamarind - 1 lemon sized or 1 big tsp tamarind paste
Ginger - 1 inch piece
Garlic - 4 cloves
Dhaniya powder - 1/2 tsp
Jeera Powder - 1/2 tsp
Dried Coconut - 2 tsp
Garam Masala - 1/4 tsp
Turmeric - 1/4 tsp
Mustard - 1 tsp
Asafoetida - 1/4 tsp
Fenugreek seeds (optional) - 1/4 tsp
Cilantro - for garnishing
Sambar powder - 1 big tsp
Chilli powder - 1/2 tsp
Rice Flour- 1 tsp
Salt to taste

Method:

  1. If using frozen pearl onions, thaw them.
  2. Soak the tamarind in warm water for 5 minutes and then squeeze out the juice. Make about 2 cups of the tamarind extract. (You can use the concentrated tamarind paste instead)
  3. Peel and finely chop ginger.
  4. Chop the tomatoes into tiny pieces. (If using fresh tomatoes)
  5. Mix the tamarind extract and tomatoes well. Add Sambar powder, chilli powder, dhaniya powder, jeera powder, dried coconut, chopped ginger to this extract and let it sit for a while.
  6. Heat 1 tsp of oil in a sauce pan, add mustard seeds and asafoetida and the fenugreek powder and let the mustard seeds splutter.
  7. Add the pearl onions and turmeric and fry the onions on medium heat until golden brown. Add the garlic cloves and let them brown.
  8. Add the tamarind extract mixture and salt. Let this mixture boil for about 15-20 minutes on medium heat until the raw smell of the masala disappears.
  9. If the Kuzhambu is little watery you can mix the rice flour with about 1/4 cup water and add this to the Kuzhambu and let it boil for 5 minutes, until it thickens.
  10. Garnish it with chopped cilantro.

Note: You can add black pepper powder to this, if you like. (This makes it really hot!)

I like to eat this with hot rice and any form of potato curry. You can use the left-over kuzhambu the next day as a side dish for idli or dosa. Try this and enjoy.
