This is a hot snack which you can make easily.

Tomato Hot Pockets

Ingredients

Pastry sheet (Pepperidge Farm's) - 1 packet. (Two sheets)
(If frozen, thaw it by putting it in the microwave. Don't overdo it.)
Tomatoes - 4
Onions - 1 big
Green Chilies - 2 or 3
Red chilly Powder - 2 tsp
Turmeric Powder - 1 tsp
Asafoetida (Hing) - two pinches
Mustard seed - 1tsp
Coriander leaves - 1 small bunch
Salt to taste
Oil enough to saute the above.

Method:

  1. Chop the onions and green chilies finely and keep aside. When the oil gets heated, put mustard seeds. When it splutters, put onions and chilly and saute well. When the onions become transparent, add the chopped tomatoes, chilly powder, turmeric powder, asafoetida and salt. Mix it well and let it cook for few minutes until the mixture boils well and turns into a thick consistency. Add chopped coriander leaves in the end.
  2. Cut the pastry sheets into small rectangles. Slightly stretch each pieces either with hand or like we make chappathis. Into each of this pieces, put two spoons of the tomato mixture and fold it with the mixture inside. Make small pockets like this out of the two pastry sheets and place it in a preheated oven (for 5 minutes at about 200 degree) and bake it for another twenty minutes at the same temperature. The pastry sheet forms a layery cover for the tomato pocket which is very tasty.
