Hai. I am Lakshmi. I live in Chicago with my Husband and 4 year old girl. For the following recipe you would need a container which is like a muffin plate but much thicker. I got it when I visited Hyderabad. It is called Ponganala peeta. Normally this dish is prepared with left over dosa dough in Rayalseema region of Andhra Pradesh. It is very tasty.

Email: LChannampalli@Pellachic.com

Guntha ponganalu

Ingredients

Dosa Dough - 2 cups
Onions - 2
Chana dal - 1/2 cup
Mint - few leaves
Cilantro(Coriander leaves)- few
Coconut - 1/2 cup
Salt - to taste
Green chillies - 4
Vegetable Oil - little
Mustard seeds- 1 spoon
Urad dal - 1 spoon

Method

  1. Prepare the dosa dough without adding too much water
  2. Soak Chana dal for 1 hour. I normally soak and Freeze so that it is convenient.
  3. In a vessel add little oil. Put mustard seeds, urad dal. When the mustard seeds begin to splutter add green chillies, onions, mint, cilantro, coconut, chana dal, salt and mix well and let it cook for 3 to 4 minutes.
  4. Add all this mixture to Dosa dough
  5. To the container called ponganala peeta put a drop in each cup and add the above mixed dough. cover it and cook it for 5 minutes. Turn it to the other side and cook for a minute and serve them.

Note:
Although they can be eaten without chutney, it tastes delicious when served with coconut chutney.
