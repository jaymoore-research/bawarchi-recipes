Pepper-Onion Rice

This recipe will go well with any 'tari-wali sabzi like aloo mattar, mattar paneer, malai kofta, chole or even plain raita

Preparation Time: 20-25 min

Ingredients

Basmati Rice 1 cup
Green/ Red/Yellow/Orange pepper 1 medium, cut into thin long pieces
(Can also use the peppers in combination but make sure that the total amount is about that of 1 medium pepper)
Onion 1 medium, cut into thin long slices
(Pink onions look particularly good!)
Cumin seeds (jeera) 1 teaspoonful
Cooking Oil (can be vegetable/canola/ even olive oil) 1 tablespoonful
chilli powder 1/2- 3/4 teaspoon
salt to taste

Method

  1. Wash the rice well with water and bring it to a boil with two cups of water, then reduce heat to medium / low and cover and cook until done ( about 10 min).
  2. Then, heat the oil in a heavy-bottomed wok and crackle the cumin seeds and then add the onions and fry until transparent.
  3. Add the peppers and fry another 2-4 min on high heat.
  4. Add salt just enough for the vegetables and mix well.
  5. Add the cooked (separated) rice to the vegetables and mix well.
  6. Add the chilli powder and salt to taste and mix well.
  7. Can also add about half a cup of cooked sweet corn or pomegranate seeds finally to make the rice look colorful.
  8. I like to finish it off with some small cherry/plum tomatoes sliced in half arranged on top of the rice in a colorful flat dish.

Note: I came up with this recipe one night out of a dire need to get a quick dinner ready for guests I had forgotten I had invited in the first place! They relished it. Hope you will too!
