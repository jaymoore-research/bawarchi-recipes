The recipe I have given below is a unique one that was perhaps "created" by my mother, but it is probably derived from the many recipes out there, with a little bit taken from each one.
The recipe makes good "sambhar" in a short time, which is very important to me as I hardly have time to spend on cooking. Eaten with rice, it makes for a nutritional meal with a South Indian flavor.

Sambhar

Ingredients

1/2 cup toor daal (2 fists or handfuls)
1 big beetroot or 2 small beetroots
1 or 2 spoons Tamarind paste - do not replace with lemon juice
1 or 2 spoons sambhar powder - essential
1 pinch turmeric powder - optional
1 tsp red chilli powder - optional
2 green chillies - optional

For the seasoning (Tadka etc):

2 tsp oil - sunflower, refined or any other
1/2 tsp mustard seeds
Curry leaves (Kadi Patta) - optional

Method:

  1. Place the toor daal and the quartered beetroot in a pressure cooker, add sufficient water and pressure cook (min 3 whistles in my experience) till the daal is completely mashed.
  2. Transfer the daal and the stock to a vessel. Take the beetroot and remove the outer peel (easier after pressure cooking) and then cut the beetroot into thin slices/quadrants.
  3. The beetroot may also be diced into small cubes, but I have found it works best with thin slices as the flavor of the beetroot is more readily extracted with thin slices than with cubes.
  4. Put the beetroot along with the dal, and boil on medium to low flame for 10 to 15 mins to allow the beetroot to cook. Depending on what consistency is desired, more or less water may be added. Adding more daal will also make the sambhar thicker.
  5. Add the tamarind paste after dissolving in a little water to ensure no lumps are left behind. 2 spoons tamarind paste will make it reasonably sour, and this is the way I guess south Indians prefer it, but if you do not like it that way, 1 spoon will suffice.
  6. Add the turmeric powder, chilli powder, green chilli slit vertically and the sambhar powder. Add salt to taste. Adding more chillies and sambhar powder will make it spicier so you can decide how much to add.
  7. Finally, place the mustard seeds and the kadi patta in a shallow pan etc, and add the oil. Heat till it splutters and then add it to the sambhar. The sambhar is now ready...

Serving Suggestion

Hot sambhar served with rice makes for a good meal on a cold day. Papads and pickle or chutney powder on the side would be even better. Enjoy your meal. Like most daal dishes, this dish has little or no fat, has proteins from the daal, and is unique because the beetroot makes the sambhar red in color !!!

Preparation time - 30 mins including pressure cooker time
Servings - makes 2 large servings
