Squash in a Flash

Ingredients

Chayote Squash (Chow-Chow Or Bangalore Kathirikkai in Tamil), peeled and grated
2 Tomatoes, medium size, diced small
1/2 cup , diced Onion or Shallots
1/2 tsp Salt
1/2 -1 tsp Hot Pepper Powder (milakai podi in Tamil) as per your taste

For "Thalippu" (seasoning)

1-2 tsp Oil
1/2 tsp Black Mustard seeds
1 tsp Washed Black Gram Dal
1 tsp Washed Bengal Gram dal
1 sprig Curry Leaves

Method:

  1. In a non-stick pan heat 1 tsp oil over medium heat.
  2. Add onions and saute until limp.
  3. Add tomatoes and cook till they get mushy. Now add salt and the grated chow-chow (sounds better than 'chayote', don't you think?). Increase the heat, add the hot pepper powder and cook the vegetable dry turning often and exercising caution not to burn it -about 5 to 10 min.
  4. When it is cooked to your desired tenderness, reduce heat. Taste and adjust salt and hot pepper powder. Now add the "thalippu" and the dish is done.

For Thalippu: This step is the crowning glory of any South Indian stir-fry vegetable recipe and it should be done just right. This step can be done at the beginning or at the end. If it is done towards the end, the dals stay crunchy.)

In small skillet heat 1 tsp oil quite hot. Add the mustard seeds; If the oil is adequatly hot they should crackle and pop instantaneously. Add the dals at once and let them fry to golden brown. It takes only a few seconds. Then add the curry leaves, stir and turn off the heat.

To serve:

  1. Can be side dish for Rice with sambar, rasam or buttermilk.
  2. Can be eaten mixed with Plain Rice and Ghee.
  3. Can eat with Nan or Chappathi.
  4. Can be used as Sandwich Filler.
  5. Can serve as Canape on crisp cracker or toasted french bread slice topped with a dollop of sour cream.
